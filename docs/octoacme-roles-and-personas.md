# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile team ceremonies and champion continuous improvement. They guide the team in adhering to agile principles, remove impediments, and foster collaboration across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate team ceremonies including daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Remove team impediments and blockers that slow down delivery
- Coach the team on agile principles and best practices
- Protect the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Track velocity and team metrics to support predictable delivery

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Ensure adherence to agile practices and ceremonies
- Continuously improve team processes and collaboration
- Maintain sustainable pace and team morale

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and follow-up on blockers
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Regular check-ins with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Escalation of persistent impediments to leadership

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on timeline management, risk tracking, and resource planning
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog is refined and ready for sprint planning
- **Developers**: Removes blockers and coaches on agile practices
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinates on acceptance criteria and definition of done

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered interfaces and experience flows. They translate user needs and product requirements into wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications that guide implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces, workflows, and interaction patterns
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity
- Review implemented features for alignment with design specifications
- Support QA with design-related acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics
- Balance user needs with technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation details
- Usability testing reports and findings
- Design specifications and handoff documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on requirements, user needs, and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Reviews implementations and provides design guidance during development
- **QA/Testing**: Defines visual and interaction acceptance criteria
- **Data Analyst**: Uses insights to inform design decisions and validate improvements

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define success metrics, analyze outcomes, and create dashboards that inform data-driven decisions. They translate raw data into actionable insights that guide product and project strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics
- Analyze user behavior, feature adoption, and business outcomes
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Conduct A/B tests and experiments to validate hypotheses
- Identify trends, patterns, and opportunities in data
- Partner with teams to ensure proper instrumentation and data collection

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across the organization
- Provide actionable insights that drive product improvements
- Ensure accurate measurement of project outcomes
- Identify opportunities through data analysis

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews with Product Manager
- Dashboard updates and data insights reports
- Experiment results and recommendations
- Data requirements for new features

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on metric-driven decisions and feature success measurement
- **Developers**: Collaborates on instrumentation and data collection implementation
- **UX Designer**: Provides data insights to inform design decisions
- **Project Manager**: Reports on project outcomes and ROI

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release planning and execution, ensuring smooth deployments to production. They manage release timelines, documentation, and communications to minimize risk and maximize reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases across multiple teams and dependencies
- Coordinate release activities including deployments, rollbacks, and monitoring
- Manage release documentation, changelogs, and stakeholder notifications
- Conduct go/no-go decisions based on readiness criteria
- Coordinate with infrastructure and operations teams
- Track post-release metrics and incident response
- Maintain release calendars and communicate blackout periods

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, low-risk releases to production
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Ensure clear communication of release status and changes
- Continuously improve release processes and automation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning sessions with Project Manager and Developers
- Go/no-go meetings with stakeholders
- Release notes and deployment notifications
- Post-release reports and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on release timelines and dependencies
- **Developers**: Works closely on deployment readiness and technical validation
- **QA/Testing**: Ensures testing is complete before release approval
- **Product Manager**: Communicates release impact and feature availability

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and analyze business requirements, acting as a liaison between stakeholders and the technical team. They translate business needs into clear specifications and ensure delivered solutions meet stakeholder expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Create process flows, business rules, and requirement specifications
- Support User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and validation
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Maintain traceability between requirements and delivered features

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of stakeholder needs
- Bridge the gap between business and technical teams
- Deliver well-documented, testable requirements
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- User story refinement with Product Manager and Developers
- UAT coordination and acceptance validation
- Requirements documentation and traceability matrices

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on feature definition and prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Supports scope management and change control
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria
- **QA/Testing**: Defines test scenarios and validates acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders**: Acts as primary liaison for requirements and validation
